# Competitor Functionality & Gap Analysis

This document provides a comprehensive analysis of wedding planning platforms, covering Israeli competitors (relevent to Haredi, Dati-Leumi, and secular markets) and top global mobile applications, identifying core features, pricing, and system gaps.

---

## 1. Israeli Competitors

### A. Haredi & Religious Segment
*   **Simcha BeClick (שמחה בקליק):**
    *   *Type:* Web directory.
    *   *Features:* Specialized Haredi/religious vendor listings (halls with partitions, glatt-kosher caterers, Hasidic bands, modest bridal wear, wigs/sheitels). Educational articles and basic lists.
    *   *Pricing:* Free for couples (monetized by vendor advertising).
*   **Bamazal (במזל):**
    *   *Type:* Basic web directory.
    *   *Features:* Searchable index of vendors catering to religious and traditional couples.

### B. Secular & Traditional Segment
*   **mit4mit / WedPlanner:**
    *   *Type:* Web & mobile app.
    *   *Features:* Interactive checklist (task manager), budget calculator, guest list manager, digital seating arranger (drag-and-drop), digital invitations (*Azmana*), and automated digital RSVP confirmations (via SMS/WhatsApp). Strict user review moderation.
    *   *Pricing:* Free for couples.
*   **Mitchatnim (متחתנים):**
    *   *Type:* Web portal (by Zap Group).
    *   *Features:* Comprehensive vendor search with religious filtering options, articles, inspiration galleries.
*   **Easywed (איזיווד):**
    *   *Type:* Tech-enabled wedding planning agency (concierge).
    *   *Features:* Personalized consultation, budget optimization, curated vendor packages, hands-on wedding day coordination.
    *   *Pricing:* Free consultation; monetization via vendor package commission and planner markup.

## 2. Global Competitors (Top App Store & Google Play)

*   **The Knot (US):**
    *   *Features:* Unified dashboard, date-customized checklists, budget tracker, free custom wedding websites, guest list manager, integrated gift registry aggregator, vendor marketplace.
    *   *Pricing:* Free (ad-supported).
*   **WeddingWire (US):**
    *   *Features:* Deep vendor marketplace with verified reviews, checklist, guest list, budget tracker, 3D interactive seating charts.
    *   *Pricing:* Free (ad-supported).
*   **Zola (US):**
    *   *Features:* Design-forward wedding websites, highly integrated store registry (gifts, cash funds), digital guest list collector, RSVP manager.
    *   *Pricing:* Free (monetized via product sales on their registry store).
*   **Joy (withjoy.com) (US):**
    *   *Features:* Guest-experience focused. Custom wedding app for guests, digital RSVP with custom questionnaire (diets, travel), live photo-sharing stream for guests, interactive event schedule.
    *   *Pricing:* Free (paid premium website themes).
*   **Bridebook (UK):**
    *   *Features:* Highly organized digital planning assistant, interactive task list, budget tracker, vendor directory.
    *   *Pricing:* Free with premium upgrades.

---

## 3. Pricing Models Summary

*   **B2C Directories (The Knot, WeddingWire, Mitchatnim, mit4mit):** 100% Free for couples. Revenue is generated from vendors paying for sponsored placements, premium listings, or banner ads.
*   **Registry-Commission (Zola, Joy):** Free tools. Revenue is generated from a percentage of physical gifts purchased through their registry store or transaction fees on cash registry funds.
*   **Concierge/Agency (Easywed):** Packages are marked up, or a flat planning fee is charged.

## 4. Key Functional Gaps (Opportunities for Margan)

Existing competitors fail to address the highly specific demands of religious Jewish weddings, presenting a major market opportunity for **Margan**:

1.  **Multi-Party Coordination:** No app supports collaborative budgeting and checklists between three separate parties (Groom's family, Bride's family, and the Couple) with customized split rules and private ledgers.
2.  **Early Planning Stages:** Competitors start planning *after* the wedding date is set. Margan begins at the matchmaking (*Shidduch*), *Vort*, and *Erusin* stages, which take place months earlier and involve complex logistics (contract signing, plate breaking, specific gifts).
3.  **Halachic Date Validation:** No global app dynamically checks dates against the Hebrew calendar to avoid prohibited periods (Sefirat HaOmer, Three Weeks, Shabbat, Jewish Holidays).
4.  **Orthodox Logistics:** Competitors do not support gender-split guest lists (necessary for separate seating and meal counts), double kitchenware shopping lists (dairy vs. meat kosher sets), or separate bed purchases for *Niddah* compliance.
5.  **Post-Wedding Week Integration:** No app manages the *Sheva Brachot* week (coordinating 7 dinners with different hosts) or local neighborhood guest lodging maps for the *Shabbat Sheva Brachot*.
