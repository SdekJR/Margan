# Monetization Strategy (Deferred)

## Current Decision
**Monetization is intentionally out of scope for the initial releases.** V1 is free for all participants: the priority is adoption inside a tight-knit, trust-driven community and validation of the core planning value.

This document records the options under consideration so that architectural decisions (vendor accounts, booking flows, ad surfaces) do not accidentally close them off.

---

## Future Options Under Consideration

### Option 1: Couple Subscription
*   A subscription fee paid by the couple (or the families) for the period they actively use the platform — naturally bounded by the wedding journey (typically 3–6 months from Vort to Sheva Brachot).
*   *Implication to keep open:* time-boxed plan lifecycle, trial/paid tier boundaries, family-level (not per-user) billing.

### Option 2: Vendor Booking Commission
*   A percentage or fixed fee charged to service providers (*sapakim*) for orders received through the platform.
*   *Implication to keep open:* bookings must be attributable to the platform (tracked quote→booking funnel), vendor accounts must exist (v2), and the vendor directory must remain credible (clear separation between organic ranking and paid placement).

### Option 3: Advertising & Sponsored Placements
*   Vendor advertising, sponsored listings, and category sponsorships — the standard model of competitor directories (see [competitor_functionality.md](./competitor_functionality.md)).
*   *Implication to keep open:* ad surfaces must respect community sensibilities (content vetting) and never compromise the neutrality of halachic/administrative guidance.

---

## Principles for the Future Decision
1.  Planning data entered by families is never sold or shared with vendors without explicit consent.
2.  Halachic and administrative guidance (date validator, Rabbinate checklists) stays free and neutral regardless of the model.
3.  Any paid placement in the vendor directory must be visibly labeled.
