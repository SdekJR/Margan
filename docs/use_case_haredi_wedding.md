# Use Case: Haredi Wedding Organization

## Domain Context
Haredi (Ultra-Orthodox Jewish) weddings are deeply rooted in religious law (Halacha) and communal traditions. They feature distinct characteristics that make planning highly complex:
1.  **Early Planning Phases:** Planning begins long before the wedding itself, starting with matchmaking (*Shidduch*) finalization and engagement agreements (*Vort* / *Tna'im*).
2.  **Two-Sided Coordination:** The process involves two independent families (the Groom's side and the Bride's side). Some tasks and budgets are shared, while others are strictly separated and assigned to one side.
3.  **Compressed Timelines:** The period between the formal engagement (*Vort*) and the wedding day is typically very short, often only 2 to 3 months.
4.  **Multi-Party Funding & Reconciliation:** Financial responsibility is shared among the groom's parents, the bride's parents, and the couple, requiring detailed splitting rules and final cost reconciliation.
5.  **Strict Religious Constraints:** Vendor selection must respect dietary laws (*Kashrut* supervision levels), gender separation, and calendar limitations (weddings are forbidden on Shabbat, holidays, and specific mourning periods).
6.  **High Attendance:** Communal obligations lead to large guest lists (often 400 to 1,000+ guests), requiring high-capacity venues.

---

## User Personas

### 1. The Coordinators (Parents of the Bride & Groom)
*   **Role:** Usually the primary financial sponsors and logistical managers.
*   **Needs:** To split costs transparently, manage massive guest lists (divided into bride's side, groom's side, and joint community guests), and negotiate with high-standard kosher vendors.
*   **Frustrations:** Communication gaps between families regarding budget shares, tracking who is paying for what, and managing separate men/women RSVPs.

### 2. The Couple (Chosson & Kallah)
*   **Role:** The focus of the celebration, managing personal and spiritual preparations.
*   **Needs:** To track personal tasks (attending preparatory classes, obtaining certificates, modesty fittings) and coordinate their guest list (friends).
*   **Frustrations:** Overwhelm from coordinating many moving parts while attending religious lessons and preparing spiritually.

## Planning Chronology (Stages)
Haredi wedding planning is split into distinct chronological phases, each with its own set of joint and side-specific tasks:

### Phase 1: Pre-Engagement & Shidduch Finalization (Months before)
*   **Joint:** Confirming the match (*Bashert*), discussing initial financial split expectations.
*   **Bride's Side / Groom's Side:** Family consultations and background checks.

### Phase 2: The Engagement & Betrothal (Vort & Erusin) (2-3 Months before)
This phase consists of two distinct events: the small Vort announcement and the larger Erusin (Tna'im) celebration.

*   **Sub-stage 2A: The Vort (Immediate Small Celebration)**
    *   See the [Detailed Vort Stage Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_vort_stage.md) for details.
    *   **Groom's Side (Primary Host):** Hosts the event at home/synagogue, buys refreshments, prints the *Meurasim* poster, purchases the engagement gift for the bride (jewelry), and schedules the *Meurasim* notice in community newspapers.
    *   **Bride's Side:** Arrives as guests; prepares their gift for the groom.

*   **Sub-stage 2B: The Erusin / Tna'im Celebration (Large Event ~200 Guests)**
    *   See the [Detailed Erusin Stage Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_erusin_stage.md) for details.
    *   **Bride's Side (Primary Host):** Books a hall for 200 people with a gender partition (*Mechitza*), coordinates catering, basic music/sound system, photographer, shuttle transport for Yeshiva/Seminary friends, and drafts the invitation (*Azmana*). Purchases the customized Talmud (*Shas*) for the groom.
    *   **Groom's Side:** Prepares the physical *Shtar Tna'im* document and the ceramic plate for breaking. Purchases formal clothing for siblings, coordinates travel to the bride's city, and invites Yeshiva rabbis/friends.

### Phase 3: Reciprocal Shabbat Visits & Marriage Registration (Post-Erusin)
*   See the [Detailed Reciprocal Shabbat Visits Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_reciprocal_shabbats.md) for logistics on hosting guest accommodations and gifts.
*   See the [Detailed Rabbinate Marriage Registration Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_rabanut_registration.md) for opening the marriage file (*Tik Nisuin*) immediately after Erusin.
*   **Joint (Administrative Prep):** Opening the marriage file at the Rabbinate, gathering required ID/parental documents, Rabbinical verification letters, paying fees, and coordinating witnesses.
*   **Groom's Visit (Hosted by Bride's Side):**
    *   **Bride's Side:** Sourcing and booking overnight lodging for the groom nearby; stocking his room with refreshments (*Kibbud*).
    *   **Groom's Side:** Buying and bringing family gifts for the bride, her parents, and siblings; coordinating travel to her city.
*   **Bride's Visit (Hosted by Groom's Side):**
    *   **Groom's Side:** Sourcing and booking overnight lodging for the bride nearby; stocking her room with refreshments (*Kibbud*).
    *   **Bride's Side:** Buying and bringing family gifts for the groom, his parents, and siblings; coordinating travel to his city.

### Phase 4: The Prep Period (1-2 Months before)
*   See the [Detailed Wedding Preparation Stage Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_wedding_prep_stage.md) for specifics on vendor bookings and mass invitations.
*   See the [Detailed Couple Meetings & Ritual Preparations Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_couple_meetings_and_ritual_preps.md) for specifics on private meetings, gifts, letters, and ritual items (Ketubah & Ring).
*   See the [Detailed Apartment Sourcing & Married Life Essentials Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_apartment_and_living_prep.md) for renting the apartment, buying furniture, appliances, double kitchenware sets, and Jewish books.
*   **Joint (Wedding Technical & Social Prep):**
    *   Aligning on the wedding city and booking the wedding hall.
    *   Selecting the catering vendor, tasting, and choosing specific dishes.
    *   Determining the music setup: choosing a music package vs. hiring a sound technician, orchestra (*Tizmoret*), and singer separately. Arranging Chuppah music.
    *   Hiring the photographer, videographer, and guest magnet maker.
    *   Optionally hiring a hall decorator/styling firm.
    *   Printing and distributing mass invitations, followed by a collaborative RSVP calling campaign to verify attendance.
    *   Scheduling and planning the limited meetings between the bride and groom, along with reciprocal gift and letter exchanges for each meeting.
*   **Joint (Living Prep):**
    *   Sourcing and renting an apartment (starting 1.5+ months before the wedding using search tools).
    *   Purchasing and scheduling delivery/assembly of essential furniture (2 separate beds/mattresses, dining table/chairs, wardrobe, bookcase) and core appliances (fridge, washing machine, gas stove/oven combo).
    *   Sourcing dual kosher kitchenware sets (separate cutlery, cookware, and tableware sets for meat and dairy), household linens, and a basic Jewish library of books.
*   **Bride's Side:** Purchasing the wedding gown, trousseau (linens/underwear), wig (*sheitel*), and head coverings (*kisuyei rosh*), booking the *Kallah* (bride) teacher, buying household items, and preparing gifts/letters for meetings.
*   **Groom's Side:** 
    *   Purchasing the groom's wedding attire (suit, hat, shoes, frock coat/*frak*, formal shirts) and underwear/linens for married life.
    *   Booking the *Shabbat Chatan* (Aufruf) venue and catering, buying the *Talit*, and booking the *Chosson* (groom) teacher.
    *   Preparing gifts/letters for meetings, purchasing the pre-wedding gifts, and sourcing the wedding day jewelry for the bride.
    *   Sourcing the halachically valid *Tabat Erusin* (plain gold ring) with his own funds and preparing the physical *Shtar Ketubah* document matching their community's *Nusach* (custom).

### Phase 5: Pre-Wedding Week & Shabbat Chatan
*   **Bride's Side:** Bridal events, final adjustments.
*   **Groom's Side:** Hosting the *Shabbat Chatan* (Aufruf) where the groom is called to the Torah, followed by communal festive meals.

### Phase 6: The Wedding Day & Sheva Brachot Week
*   See the [Detailed Sheva Brachot Stage Use Case](file:///c:/Users/shimo/OneDrive/Projects/Margan/docs/use_case_sheva_brachot_stage.md) for specifics on post-wedding meals and Shabbat logistics.
*   **Joint (The Wedding Day):**
    *   The wedding ceremony (*Chuppah*) and reception.
    *   **Bride's Side / Groom's Side:** Coordinating separate guest receptions (*Kabbalat Panim* for Kallah, *Tisch* for Chatan) and managing their respective guest lists.
*   **Post-Wedding (Sheva Brachot Week):**
    *   **Joint:** Coordinating the scheduling, venues, and custom guest lists for the seven days of post-wedding festive meals (*Sheva Brachot*), which can be hosted by either family or external hosts.
    *   **Bride's Side (Shabbat Sheva Brachot Lead):** Organizing the major *Shabbat Sheva Brachot* in the bride's city: renting a dining venue if the home is too small, preparing the multi-meal Shabbat menu, and sourcing and allocating overnight lodging for the groom's family and out-of-town guests.

## Core Planning Workflow & Agent Use Cases

### 1. Automated Calendar & Date Validator
*   **What the system does:** The user inputs a desired timeframe. The system integrates a Jewish calendar engine to identify Halachically permissible wedding dates, automatically filtering out prohibited times (Shabbat, holidays, Sefirat HaOmer, the Three Weeks) and highlighting auspicious dates.
*   **Why:** Calculating permissible dates according to the Hebrew calendar is complex. A mistake can lead to venue deposits being lost or a Rabbi refusing to officiate.

### 2. Collaborative Task Manager (Shared vs. Split Tasks)
*   **What the system does:** Categorizes tasks into "Joint" (visible and editable by both families/couple) and "Side-Specific" (visible only to the groom's side or bride's side, e.g., planning the *Shabbat Chatan* or bridal gown fitting). It allows parents and the couple to track progress independently while feeding into a unified milestone dashboard.
*   **Why:** Each side has private logistics and cultural events to manage, but the final timeline depends on all tasks being completed.

### 3. Multi-Party Budget & Financial Reconciliation
*   **What the system does:** Allows all three parties (Bride's family, Groom's family, Couple) to manage a shared budget. Users assign items to:
    *   *Shared:* Split by percentage (e.g., 50/50, 60/40) or fixed amounts.
    *   *Side-Specific:* Paid entirely by one side (e.g., Groom's side pays for the band, Bride's side pays for the dowry/furniture).
    *   The agent maintains a real-time ledger of who paid what and automatically calculates a final reconciliation (e.g., "Groom's family must transfer $X to Bride's family to settle shared hall costs").
*   **Why:** Traditional agreements involve complex cost divisions. Automated reconciliation eliminates manual calculations and potential friction during settlements.

### 4. Specialized Vendor Matchmaking
The agent filters and recommends vendors based on strict Orthodox standards:
*   **Venues:** Must feature separate entrances or large spaces suitable for gender-separated receptions (*Kabbalat Panim* for the bride, *Tisch* for the groom), a partition (*Mechitza*) for separate dancing, and a private room (*Yichud Room*) for the couple.
*   **Caterers:** Must offer specific, trusted *Hechsheirim* (Kosher certifications) requested by the families (e.g., *Badatz Eda Charedit*, *Rubin*, *Landau*).
*   **Attire:** Modest wedding gown rental services (*Gmachs* or specialized designers) that adhere to *Tznius* standards.
*   **Musicians:** Bands capable of playing traditional Hasidic or Yeshivish wedding sets, matching the family's specific community subgroup.

### 5. Separate-Seating RSVP & Guest Manager
*   **What the system does:** Manages invitations and RSVPs while categorizing guests by gender (Men/Women) and relation. It generates separate seating charts for the men's section and the women's section and automatically adjusts the count of meals required for each side.
*   **Why:** Traditional seating is strictly separated. Having an accurate, gender-segregated headcount is critical for hall setup, table counts, and catering orders.

### 6. Ribbons of Honor (*Kibbudim*) Planner
*   **What the system does:** Helps the couple and their families assign specific ceremonial honors (e.g., being called to witness the Ketubah, reciting one of the *Sheva Brachot* under the Chuppah or during the celebratory week). The agent keeps track of who has been assigned which honor and sends them details about their role.
*   **Why:** Assigning honors is a sensitive social process in Haredi communities. Forgetting an honor or double-assigning can cause social friction.

### 7. The Post-Wedding *Sheva Brachot* Coordinator
*   **What the system does:** Coordinates the seven days of festive meals following the wedding. The agent helps schedule who is hosting each night, manages guest lists for each specific night, and tracks catering or meal prep details.
*   **Why:** Organizing seven consecutive days of mini-events immediately after the main wedding is exhausting. The agent automates the scheduling and guest tracking so the family can focus on celebrating.
