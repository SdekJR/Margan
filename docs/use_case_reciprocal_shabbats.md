# Use Case: Reciprocal Shabbat Visits (Post-Erusin)

## Overview
After the Erusin (betrothal) celebration and before the main wedding preparation period, it is customary for the bride and groom to make formal weekend visits to each other's families for a Shabbat. 

These visits consist of two distinct events:
1.  **Groom's Visit:** The groom travels to the bride's family's community for Shabbat.
2.  **Bride's Visit:** The bride travels to the groom's family's community for Shabbat.

Because these visits take place in Orthodox communities where travel on Shabbat is forbidden and modesty standards are strictly maintained, specific lodging, hosting, and gift-giving logistics are required.

---

## User Scenario 1: The Groom Visiting the Bride's Family

### 1. Host Responsibilities (Bride's Side)
*   **Lodging Allocation:** The Bride's family must find and book suitable overnight lodging for the groom within walking distance of their home (e.g., a guest apartment or a host room at a neighbor's house). Due to modesty standards (*Yichud*), the groom cannot sleep in the bride's family home if it does not have a separate, compliant guest setup.
*   **Room Hospitality (*Kibbud*):** The Bride's family must stock the groom's lodging room with refreshments, including drinks (e.g., Coca-Cola), nuts, pastries, and sweets.

### 2. Guest Responsibilities (Groom's Side)
*   **Gifts for the Host Family:** The groom must purchase and bring gifts for:
    *   The bride (e.g., jewelry or a book).
    *   The bride's parents.
    *   The bride's siblings (brothers and sisters).
*   **Travel Coordination:** Arrange transport to the bride's city before Shabbat begins.

---

## User Scenario 2: The Bride Visiting the Groom's Family

### 1. Host Responsibilities (Groom's Side)
*   **Lodging Allocation:** The Groom's family must arrange overnight lodging for the bride close to their home (similar guest housing or neighbor's apartment).
*   **Room Hospitality (*Kibbud*):** The Groom's family must stock the bride's lodging room with refreshments (drinks, nuts, sweets, and pastries).

### 2. Guest Responsibilities (Bride's Side)
*   **Gifts for the Host Family:** The bride must purchase and bring gifts for:
    *   The groom.
    *   The groom's parents.
    *   The groom's siblings (brothers and sisters).
*   **Travel Coordination:** Arrange transport to the groom's city before Shabbat begins.

---

## Key Functional Requirements

| Feature | What it does | Why it is needed |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Guest Accommodation Tracker** | Manages bookings and locations of guest rooms for the visiting bride or groom. | Ensures lodging is finalized and within walking distance of the hosts. |
| **Visiting Gift Checklist** | Generates shopping items for family gifts, divided by recipient (parents, siblings, betrothed). | Prevents overlooking gifts for family members, which is socially important. |
| **Kibbud Shopping List** | Automates a shopping checklist for room refreshments (drinks, sweets, pastries). | Ensures the host family prepares the guest room properly. |
