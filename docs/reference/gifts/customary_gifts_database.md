# Reference: Customary Gifts Database

This document catalogs the traditional and customary gifts exchanged during a Haredi engagement and wedding process. It details what is given, when it is presented, who buys it, and the cultural context behind each gift.

---

## 1. Gifts from the Groom's Side to the Bride

Traditional gifts from the groom (*Chosson*) and his family focus on jewelry and personal items for the bride (*Kallah*).

| Gift Item | Occasion | Context & Specifications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gold Watch** | Vort or Erusin | A classic engagement gift for the Kallah, symbolizing the beginning of their shared time. |
| **Diamond Engagement Ring** | Erusin | The formal ring showing betrothal. In some circles, it is presented during the Erusin party. |
| **Gold Bracelet or Necklace** | Erusin or Wedding Prep | Additional jewelry showing the groom's family's appreciation. |
| **Pearl Necklace / Earrings** | Wedding Day | Presented on the wedding day to be worn by the bride under the canopy (*Chuppah*). |
| **Leather-Bound Siddur** | Before the Wedding | A prayer book customized with the bride's new married surname printed in gold lettering. |
| **Perfume / Toiletries** | Visiting Shabbat | Small, personal gifts presented during the groom's Shabbat visits. |

---

## 2. Gifts from the Bride's Side to the Groom

Traditional gifts from the bride (*Kallah*) and her family focus on religious texts, ritual clothing, and silver Judaica for the groom (*Chosson*).

| Gift Item | Occasion | Context & Specifications |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Talmud Set (Shas)** | Erusin | A complete, leather-bound set of the Babylonian Talmud. This is the most prestigious and customary gift for a Chosson. |
| **Silver Kiddush Cup** | Vort | A silver cup for wine blessings, often used at the Vort and future Shabbat meals. |
| **Machzorim Set** | Vort or Prep Period | A set of leather-bound holiday prayer books. |
| **Tallit & Embroidered Bag** | Before the Wedding | The prayer shawl worn by the groom at the Chuppah. Usually accompanied by a custom velvet or leather bag. |
| **Kittel** | Before the Wedding | A white linen/cotton robe worn by the groom under the Chuppah. |
| **Cuff Links** | Visiting Shabbat | Silver or gold cuff links to be worn with the groom's wedding shirts. |

## 3. Reciprocal Shabbat Gifts (For the Host Families)

When the bride or groom visits the other's family for Shabbat, it is customary to bring gifts to show gratitude to the hosts.

### Groom visiting the Bride's Family
*   **For the Mother-in-Law:** A bouquet of flowers, a decorative silver-plated dish, or a high-quality table runner.
*   **For the Father-in-Law:** A bottle of fine kosher wine or a new commentary book on the Torah.
*   **For the Siblings:** Chocolate boxes, pastries, or age-appropriate religious books.

### Bride visiting the Groom's Family
*   **For the Mother-in-Law:** Flowers, a decorative centerpiece, or a specialty kitchen/baking item.
*   **For the Father-in-Law:** High-quality silver polish or a decorative Havdalah candle/spice holder.
*   **For the Siblings:** Small sweets, treats, or books.

---

## 4. Wedding Gifts from Guests

*   **Monetary Gifts:** Cash or check is the most common gift, traditionally given in multiples of **18** (e.g., $180, $360), which is the numerical value of the Hebrew word *Chai* (Life).
*   **Household Items:** Kitchenware, linens, towels, or silver Judaica (e.g., challah board, candlesticks) selected from the couple's registry.
