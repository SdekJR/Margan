# Reference: Jewish & Haredi Wedding Checklists

This document summarizes and systematizes wedding preparation checklists from various sources, focusing on both general Jewish traditions and specific Haredi (Ultra-Orthodox) requirements.

---

## 1. Timeline & Planning Milestones

### 12+ Months Out (Initial Foundation)
*   **Establish Budget & Split Rules:** Set the target budget and negotiate financial split agreements between the bride's parents, groom's parents, and the couple.
*   **Set the Wedding Date:** Verify Hebrew calendar dates to avoid prohibited times (Shabbat, major holidays, Sefirat HaOmer, Three Weeks).
*   **Book the Wedding Hall:** Secure a venue that meets separate-seating requirements (*Mechitza*) and has distinct rooms for the *Tisch*, *Kabbalat Panim*, and *Yichud* room.
*   **Secure the Officiating Rabbi:** Find an authorized Rabbi approved by the Chief Rabbinate.

### 6–9 Months Out (Vendor & Technical Bookings)
*   **Hire Key Vendors:** Book photographers, videographers, caterers, and musicians/bands familiar with Orthodox event flows.
*   **Confirm Kashrut Standards:** Verify that the caterer and venue match the exact *Hechsher* (kosher supervision level) requested by both families.
*   **Begin Attire Sourcing:** Shop for the wedding gown (ensuring modesty/*Tznius* standards) and the groom's wedding wardrobe (suits, hats, frock coat).

### 2–3 Months Out (Engagement & Initial Events)
*   **Hold the Vort:** Small engagement announcement celebration hosted by the groom's side.
*   **Hold the Erusin (Tna'im):** Large betrothal celebration (~200 guests) hosted by the bride's side, featuring the signing of the *Shtar Tna'im* and breaking of the plate.
*   **Open the Rabanut File:** Immediately after Erusin, open the marriage registration file (*Tik Nisuin*) with the Rabbinate.
*   **Reciprocal Shabbat Visits:** Coordinate the couple's Shabbat visits to each other's families, including nearby guest lodging and family gifts.

### 1.5 Months Out (Home & Logistics)
*   **Apartment Search:** Begin searching real estate portals (e.g., Yad2) for a rented apartment in the target neighborhood.
*   **Attire Fittings:** Complete fittings for the wedding gown, suits, and family clothing.
*   **Order Print Invitations:** Design and print invitations with appropriate community titles and wording (*Nusach*).

### 1 Month Out (Final Prep)
*   **Rent & Lease:** Sign the lease for the apartment.
*   **Order Furniture & Appliances:** Purchase essential furniture (separate beds/mattresses, dining set, wardrobe, bookcase) and appliances (fridge, washing machine, stove/oven) to ensure delivery 2 weeks before the wedding.
*   **Buy Household Items:** Source dual sets of kosher kitchenware (separate meat and dairy sets for cutlery, pots/pans, tableware), linens, towels, and basic Jewish books.
*   **Send Wedding Invitations:** Distribute invitations to the wider circle of friends and community.
*   **Register for Classes:** The bride attends *Kallah* teacher classes, and the groom attends *Chosson* teacher classes.

### 2 Weeks Out (Final Squeeze)
*   **Deliveries & Assembly:** Complete furniture assembly and appliance installation at the new apartment.
*   **Calling Campaign:** Run phone follow-ups for unconfirmed guest invitations to finalize RSVPs.
*   **Witnesses & Rabanut:** Send the two male witnesses to the Rabbinate office to testify.
*   **Shtar Ketubah & Ring:** Purchase the plain gold *Tabat Erusin* and prepare the physical *Ketubah* document.
*   **Wedding Jewelry:** Purchase the bridal jewelry to be gifted on the wedding day.

## 2. Checklist of Jewish Ritual Elements

| Ritual Item | Sponsoring Side | Timing / Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shtar Tna'im** | Groom's Side | Signed at Erusin; details engagement conditions. |
| **Ceramic Plate** | Groom's Side (Mother) | Broken at Erusin to seal the commitment. |
| **Shtar Ketubah** | Groom's Side | Marriage contract signed under the canopy. |
| **Tabat Erusin (Ring)** | Groom's Side | Plain gold ring purchased with groom's own funds. |
| **Tallit & Velvet Bag** | Bride's Side | Gifted to the groom; worn under the Chuppah. |
| **Kittel** | Bride's Side | White robe worn by the groom during the Chuppah. |
| **Glass for Breaking** | Groom's Side | Glass broken by the groom at the end of the ceremony. |
| **Yichud Room Key** | Hall Coordinator | Coordinates the private room seclusion immediately after the Chuppah. |
| **Kippot & Bentschers** | Joint | Custom skullcaps and grace-after-meals booklets for guests. |
